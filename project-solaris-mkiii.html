Solaris MkIII

Propulsion engineering and combustion stability

Workstream

Systems

Tools

Project

Monash High Powered Rocketry

Overview

Solaris MkIII development included combustion stability work for a liquid oxygen hybrid rocket engine. The focus was on designing and integrating components that reduce instability, based on literature review and test cycles.

Stability Solution

Helmholtz Resonators

Developed Helmholtz resonators tuned to resonant frequencies in the engine to absorb acoustic energy and mitigate combustion oscillations.

Injector-Face Baffles

Designed injector-face baffles to improve oxidiser flow distribution and reduce instability triggers at the injector face.

Fuel Inserts

Implemented fuel inserts to control regression behaviour and support stable hybrid combustion throughout the burn.

Engineering Process

The work involved completing a literature review, running PDR and CDR cycles for internal and external stakeholders, and producing CAD models and engineering drawings.

  • Review of combustion instability literature
  • Design of acoustic damping systems
  • Component integration in Creo and Windchill
  • Technical documentation and review cycles
