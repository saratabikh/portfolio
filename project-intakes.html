Resonance in Supersonic Intakes

Final Year Project • CFD, wind tunnel testing, schlieren imaging

Project

Final Year Project

Duration

February 2026 – Present

Research Group

Shock Lab

Focus

Supersonic intake resonance

Overview

This Final Year Project investigates resonance in supersonic inlets, especially the buzz mechanism that appears at overspeed conditions. The work combines CFD, supersonic wind tunnel testing and high-speed schlieren imaging to understand and characterise this phenomenon.

Computational Work

Performed CFD analysis to study inlet flow behaviour and resonance conditions, using the results to guide test article design and instrumentation placement.

Wind Tunnel Testing

Supersonic test articles

Designed and built inlet models for the Shock Lab wind tunnel, including instrumentation ports for pressure measurement.

Data acquisition

Used high-frequency pressure sensors and data acquisition systems to capture resonance frequencies and oscillation amplitudes.

Schlieren Imaging

Used high-speed schlieren imaging to visualise shock structures and flow oscillations during resonance events.
